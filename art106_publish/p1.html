Assignment 1

Human/Machine Collaboration

This assignment was asking the question as to how we, as artists, could interact and perform with an 'everday' machine. What kind of performance would we doing to make it feel as though the machine itself had a degree of autonomy and was not just a tool being used by the artist.
This assignment had 3 parts to it.
-- Create a diagram that has details of how both the artist and the machine function as a team,
-- Have a 'team photo' with your partner, something that could be the cover of you playbill,
and
-- Have an artistic critque of your performance. They can describe and project how they feel about the project.

Assignment 2

Whats in the box? Leds!

The LED assignment is comprised of multiple phases as it stretches across multiple arcs.
-- Phase 1 is our intro to creating a circuit of LEDs. We used cardboard to hold our LEDs in place while we had all the wiring and connections behind the cardboard
-- The main pull from phase 2 was using a breadboard to limit the amount of soldering. With the use of the breadboard, as well as having to solder less, it also allows the wiring to look cleaner and more organized.
-- Phase 3 introduced a switch system to our circuit. previously we had been just touching the ends of our wire to our 9V battery. Now we had a battery clip that we could hook up to
-- Phase 4 was supposed to be our final presentation of the prior 3 phases. I do not think I followed the order of phases or operations correctly as I do not think I executed the desired result. What I presented did not use a breadboard or a clothes peg switch. I had been preoccupied testing the breadboard and the copper switch concept with loose LED bulbs, making sure I was understanding how I'd be

-- I shaped my 6 LEDs into a triangle as it was an easy, manageable shape. I am weirdly quite familiar with triangles as me and a partner wrote a javascript function to make coding easier than the default js function.

-- Phase 5 is art106 MakerFaire Led Synth, a collaborative project between the whole art106 class that is to be presented at Maker Faire. The class was broken into pairs and with our partner we were to design an image out of multiple LEDs. The board has to have 4 switchable circuits in order to trigger the whole image. My partner and I both enjoyed videogames. So we wanted to make an pixel art Pac-Man.
Our design shows our whole image to be made out of 9 trios of LEDs and 2 quads and those 11 are split between 4 different switches in such a way that if only one switch is pressed(many two), your would not be able to tell what the design is. It definitely requires all 4 switches to appreciate.
